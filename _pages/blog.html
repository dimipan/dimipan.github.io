Three Refusals

On what the cell, the cave, and the front door have in common.

Socrates in his prison. Antigone in her tomb. Nora in her front hall. Three figures separated by twenty-three centuries who would, if you put them in a room, understand each other immediately. The same three-part structure of refusal — the appeal, the refusal of the game, the acceptance of the cost — in three different geometries.

The Men Who Built the Cages

Creon, Torvald, and the tragedy of the frightened king.

Five men across twenty-four centuries — Creon, Jason, Theseus, Torvald, the absent Count of Miss Julie — diagnosed with the same disease. They did not build their cages out of hatred. They built them out of materials they sincerely believed were love. And the playwrights, almost unanimously, did not punish them. They pitied them.
